Core Veneer

The fundamental strength of high-quality plywood.

What is Core Veneer?

Core Veneer is the essential inner layer, or "core," used in the manufacturing of plywood, blockboard, and other engineered wood panels. It is a thin sheet of wood, typically 1mm to 3mm thick, that is rotary-peeled from a log.

The quality, strength, and durability of the core veneer directly determine the final quality of the plywood. At Oswal Lumbers, we manufacture two primary types of high-strength core veneer to meet the rigorous demands of the industry: **Eucalyptus Core** and **Pine Core**.

Our state-of-the-art peeling and drying process ensures uniform thickness, optimal moisture content, and a smooth surface, providing a superior raw material for plywood manufacturers.

Our Core Veneer Products

We offer two distinct types of core veneer, each with its own unique advantages, to suit your specific manufacturing needs.

Eucalyptus Core Veneer (Euca Core)

Known for its exceptional strength and high density, Eucalyptus veneer is the preferred choice for manufacturing high-quality, durable plywood. It provides excellent structural integrity and a borer-proof, warp-resistant final product.

Key Features:

  • High Density & Superior Strength
  • Excellent Dimensional Stability
  • Natural Resistance to Borers & Termites
  • Ideal for Structural & Calibrated Plywood

Available Thicknesses:

1.20 mm 1.80 mm 2.20 mm 2.50 mm

Pine Core Veneer

Sourced from sustainably managed plantations, Pine Core Veneer is lighter in weight and offers a clear, uniform surface. It is easy to work with and provides excellent bonding capabilities, making it a versatile choice for a wide range of plywood applications.

Key Features:

  • Lightweight & Easy to Handle
  • Clear, Knot-Free Surface
  • Excellent Glue Adhesion
  • Perfect for General Purpose & MR Grade Plywood

Available Thicknesses:

1.20 mm 1.80 mm 2.20 mm 2.50 mm
